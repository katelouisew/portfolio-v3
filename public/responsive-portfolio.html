Responsive Portfolio Design & Development

Client: Brisbane-based Artist, Kristian Fracchia



One of my first freelance web design projects was for a young artist named Kristian. He wanted a space online to professionally exhibit his body of work, in addition to sharing his art on Facebook and Instagram. As Kristian's work consisted of sketches and drawings done in greyscale, he wanted his website to reflect this minimalistic style.

Kristian wanted to update the website himself, adding images to the portfolio gallery, new exhibition folders and updating his biography / CV. To make this possible, we used Wordpress as the content management system. This was a great opportunity for me to familiarise myself with the Wordpress platform, and it allowed Kristian to have more control over his website in the future.

As a newcomer to web development my knowledge of RWD (responsive web design) at the time was fairly limited. Through research and taking on additional web design projects I have learnt about RWD and the techniques involved with making websites responsive for a variety of browser sizes. I am currently in the process of fixing this issue with Kristian's website, and will update this page with a link when it's complete.
