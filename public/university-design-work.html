Design Projects at University

Bachelor of Multimedia Design



In 2012 I commenced studying a Bachelor of Multimedia Design at the University of Queensland (UQ). I had contemplated studying graphic design at a college specialising in the creative industries, but was tempted by the technical aspect of the Multimedia Design degree offered at UQ.

During my first year at university I took an 'Introduction to Web Design' course, that focused on building interactive websites with basic functionality. I learnt the foundations of HTML, CSS and JavaScript - continuing to study these topics in other courses later in my degree. Other classes included programming with Python, relational databases, discrete mathematics and a number of design courses.

My favourite design related course was Graphic Design, in which students learnt about visual communication with the Adobe Suite. I also completed several studio courses that saw students from related degrees (IT, Multimedia Design, Computer Science etc) working together in teams to create webapps, websites and even games. During these pieces of assessment I worked primarily as a designer and project manager, initialising meetings and assisting communication between technical and design-focused team members. The last group project was in Physical Computing & Interaction Studio. In this double-weighted unit we learnt about sensor and display technologies used to gather input through human interaction.

Throughout my degree I learnt a number of practical design skills and added to my understanding of design principles. In addition to improving my communication skills by working as part of a team and leading projects, I had the opportunity to work with people from different disciplines. The variety of areas I studied during my degree has allowed me to refine my goals and choose a specific career path that I aim to follow.
